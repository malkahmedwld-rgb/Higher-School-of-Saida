Reaching Struggling Students

Understanding, Identifying & Supporting Learners

Students Cherifi Leila Nour El houda & Djellouli Allaa Abir
Supervisor Ms. Azzeddine Bencherab
View Presentation View Research Paper

Introduction

Some students face academic or behavioral challenges that affect their learning and classroom environment. Recognizing their unique needs and providing early support is essential to help them succeed.

Definition

A struggling student rarely turs completed assignments, poor quiz/test results, might work longer than others, makes very little progress.

Impact on students' studies

  • 1. Low motivation
  • 2. Low confidence
  • 3. Risk of failure
  • 4. Dropping out of school

Why do students struggle in schools?

  • Ineffective teaching
  • Lack of differentiation
  • Family problem
  • Financial stress
  • Trauma/ Bullying

Why should teachers intervene?

  • Encourage inclusion
  • Build strong relationships
  • Improve classroom environment
  • Prevent future failures

How to identify struggling students?

  • Consistent below-average performance
  • Disengagement
  • Incomplete work
  • Withdrawal or frustration

How can teachers help?

Teachers can use various methods to support their learners by:

  • using charts and pictures
  • using differentiated instruction (VAK)
  • providing small group support
  • giving immediate feedback
  • encouraging and motivating them
  • involving parents

Conclusion

Struggling students require early identification & structured support as effective interventions benefit: the student, the family, and the school community.
